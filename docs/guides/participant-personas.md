# Freedi Participant Personas

## Persona 1: Maya, The Frustrated Community Member

- **Age**: 34, urban planner assistant
- **Pain**: Feels her voice is lost in town hall meetings. The loud voices always win.
- **What's in it for her**: Finally a way to contribute her ideas without being interrupted. She can think, write, and evaluate others' ideas at her own pace. Her good idea gets recognized by consensus, not by volume.
- **Key motivation**: **Equality of voice**
- **Behavior**: Writes detailed suggestions, carefully evaluates others, checks back to see how her ideas rank

**Nano-banana prompt:**

## Persona 2: Oren, The Overwhelmed Manager

- **Age**: 42, team lead at a tech company (15 people)
- **Pain**: Every team meeting turns into a debate dominated by 3 people. Decisions feel forced. Half the team disengages.
- **What's in it for him**: He can get EVERYONE's real opinion asynchronously. He sees what the team actually thinks, not just what the loudest say. Better decisions = better outcomes = he looks good.
- **Key motivation**: **Better team decisions with less friction**
- **Behavior**: Creates deliberations, reviews consensus results, uses data to make final calls

**Nano-banana prompt:**

> Portrait of a 42-year-old man with short dark hair and light stubble, wearing a button-down shirt with rolled sleeves, sitting at a desk with multiple screens, one hand on chin thinking, tired but engaged eyes, modern office background, illustration style, blue and gray tones

---

## Persona 3: Noa, The Shy Student

- **Age**: 19, university student in political science
- **Pain**: Has strong opinions but freezes in class discussions. Her ideas are good on paper but she can't articulate them live.
- **What's in it for her**: She can express herself through writing. She sees others agree with her ideas. It builds her confidence. She discovers she's actually persuasive when given the right medium.
- **Key motivation**: **A safe space to express and be heard**
- **Behavior**: Writes thoughtful suggestions, reads everything, evaluates carefully, rarely initiates but deeply engages

**Nano-banana prompt:**

> Portrait of a 19-year-old young woman with straight black hair and glasses, wearing a university hoodie, sitting cross-legged on a library floor with a laptop, concentrated expression with a slight smile, books and warm lighting in background, illustration style, soft pastel tones

---

## Persona 4: Rami, The Skeptical Citizen

- **Age**: 55, retired engineer
- **Pain**: Doesn't trust institutions. Has seen too many "consultations" that were just rubber stamps. Thinks nobody really listens.
- **What's in it for him**: Transparency. He can SEE the process. He can see all options, see how people voted, verify the outcome isn't rigged. The math is visible. If enough people agree with him - it shows.
- **Key motivation**: **Transparency and accountability**
- **Behavior**: Reads everything critically, evaluates strongly (both agree and disagree), watches results closely, vocal if process seems unfair

**Nano-banana prompt:**

> Portrait of a 55-year-old man with gray hair and reading glasses pushed up on forehead, weathered face with sharp intelligent eyes, wearing a simple polo shirt, arms crossed, standing in a public square, skeptical but attentive expression, illustration style, earthy natural tones

---

## Persona 5: Lina, The Community Organizer

- **Age**: 28, works at an NGO focused on youth empowerment
- **Pain**: She organizes workshops but can never scale beyond 30 people. Post-its and whiteboards don't capture the nuance. Reporting results is manual and painful.
- **What's in it for her**: She can run deliberations with 300 people instead of 30. Results are automatic. She gets data she can show funders. Participants feel more engaged because it's on their phone.
- **Key motivation**: **Scale and impact**
- **Behavior**: Creates many deliberations, designs question structures carefully, shares links widely, presents results to stakeholders

**Nano-banana prompt:**

> Portrait of a 28-year-old woman with short dyed red hair and an earring, wearing a colorful scarf and denim jacket, energetic smile, holding a phone in one hand and gesturing with the other, community garden or co-working space background, vibrant and dynamic, illustration style, warm energetic colors

## Persona 6: David, The Time-Strapped Parent

- **Age**: 38, works full-time, two kids, active in school parent committee
- **Pain**: Can't attend evening meetings. Misses decisions that affect his kids. Feels guilty and disconnected.
- **What's in it for him**: He can participate from his phone at 10pm after the kids sleep. 5 minutes to read options and vote. He's included without rearranging his life.
- **Key motivation**: **Asynchronous participation - involvement without time sacrifice**
- **Behavior**: Short bursts of engagement, reads quickly, evaluates, rarely writes long suggestions but votes on everything

**Nano-banana prompt:**

> Portrait of a 38-year-old man with tired but kind eyes, slight beard, wearing a casual sweater, sitting on a couch at night with a phone, dim warm lamp light, toys visible in background, relaxed but focused expression, illustration style, warm nighttime tones with soft glow

## Summary: What's In It For Them

| Persona | Core Need | Freedi Value Proposition |
|---------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Maya** (Community Member) | Equal voice | Ideas win by quality, not volume |
| **Oren** (Manager) | Real team input | Async consensus reveals true opinions |
| **Noa** (Shy Student) | Safe expression | Writing > speaking for introverts |
| **Rami** (Skeptic) | Transparency | Open process, visible results |
| **Lina** (Organizer) | Scale | 30 to 300 participants, automatic results |
| **David** (Busy Parent) | Flexibility | 5 min at 10pm = full participation |

## Cross-Cutting Themes (What ALL Personas Get)

1. **Agency** - "My voice actually counts"
2. **Fairness** - "The process is equal for everyone"
3. **Convenience** - "I participate on my terms"
4. **Clarity** - "I can see what everyone thinks and where consensus is"
5. **Impact** - "The results lead to real decisions"
