## Part 1: Business Plan

#### Strengths (Internal)
- **Deep Expertise**: Creator has unique combination of philosophy, cognitive science, software development, and deliberation facilitation

- **Solo Operation**: Founder doing everything (development, sales, facilitation, support)

- **Growing Demand**: Increased interest in participatory democracy and citizen engagement

- **Competition**: Pol.is, Decidim, Loomio, Remesh, and other deliberation platforms

### 1.3 Market Segments & Positioning

#### Primary Segments (Year 1 Focus)

**1. Academic Research Institutions** üéì

- **Pain Points**: Expensive proprietary tools, lack of customization, limited transparency
- **Value Prop**: Open-source, academically rigorous, export data, modify algorithms
- **Pricing**: Free for research + paid consulting/training
- **Examples**: University deliberation labs, civic innovation centers, political science departments

**2. Political Campaigns & Parties** üó≥Ô∏è
- **Needs**: Voter engagement, policy co-creation, constituency feedback
- **Pain Points**: Low engagement, expensive polling, disconnection from voters
- **Value Prop**: Authentic engagement, consensus-finding, visible impact
- **Pricing**: Project-based (30,000-100,000 NIS per campaign)
- **Examples**: Chris/Dan in US, Israeli political parties, municipal campaigns

**3. Marketing Research Companies** üìä
- **Needs**: Focus groups, market research, consumer feedback at scale
- **Pain Points**: Expensive traditional methods, limited sample sizes, slow insights
- **Value Prop**: Scalable deliberation, rich qualitative data, consensus metrics
- **Pricing**: White-label or API access (5,000-20,000 NIS/month)
- **Examples**: Tomer Avital's firm, market research agencies

**4. Civic Organizations & Communities** üèòÔ∏è
- **Needs**: Participatory budgeting, community decisions, resilience building
- **Pain Points**: Low participation, polarization, decision gridlock
- **Value Prop**: Inclusive, scalable, proven methodology
- **Pricing**: Freemium (free < 100 users, paid for larger)
- **Examples**: Fourth Quarter project, Karkur community, neighborhood councils

#### Secondary Segments (Year 2+)

**5. Corporations** üè¢
- **Use Cases**: Employee engagement, innovation jams, strategic planning
- **Value Prop**: Better decisions, employee voice, transparent process
- **Pricing**: Enterprise licenses (10,000-50,000 NIS/month)

**6. International NGOs & Development** üåç
- **Use Cases**: Peacebuilding, development programs, stakeholder engagement

### 1.4 Positioning Statement

**For** researchers, civic leaders, and organizations seeking to make better collective decisions at scale,

**FreeDi** is an open-source deliberative democracy platform

**That** combines proven consensus algorithms with structured facilitation to enable hundreds of participants to find common ground on complex issues,

**Unlike** proprietary tools like Pol.is or Remesh,

**FreeDi** offers full transparency, academic rigor, and customization while remaining free and open-source.

**Tagline**: *"Open-source democracy at scale"*

### 1.8 Team & Partnerships

#### Current Reality (Solo Operation)
**Strengths**:
- Full control, fast decisions
- No revenue sharing
- Deep product knowledge

**Weaknesses**:
- Overwhelm, burnout risk
- Can't scale sales and delivery
- Limited time for development

#### Partnership Considerations

**Option A: Continue Solo (with contractors)**
- Hire freelance sales/marketing help (10-20 hours/week)
- Use contractors for specific projects
- Keep full ownership and control
- **Best if**: Want to maintain vision, not ready for partners

**Option B: Partnership with Osnat**
- She handles business development, sales, strategy
- You handle product, technology, facilitation
- Revenue split (60/40 or 50/50?)
- **Best if**: Need business expertise, ready to share control
- **Critical**: Clear roles, decision-making process, exit terms

**Option C: Hire Employees**
- Sales/BD person (junior, grows into role)
- Part-time marketing/content person
- Keep ownership, build team culture
- **Best if**: Have stable revenue, clear hiring criteria

**Recommendation** (based on diary insights):
Start with **Option A** (solo + contractors) until revenue reaches 30,000 NIS/month consistently. This gives you:
- Time to build systems and processes
- Clarity on what roles you truly need
- Leverage without commitment
- Ability to "test" Osnat on specific projects before full partnership

### 2.1 Marketing Strategy Overview

**Core Insight**: FreeDi is not a typical SaaS product. It's a movement, a methodology, and a platform. Marketing must build credibility, educate, and inspire before selling.

**Primary Audiences**:
1. **Academic Researchers** (early adopters, credibility builders)
2. **Deliberation Practitioners** (facilitators, consultants, civic innovators)
3. **Democracy Enthusiasts** (activists, NGOs, civic hackers)
4. **Decision-Makers** (politicians, executives, community leaders)

**Marketing Philosophy**:
- **Open & Transparent**: Share everything (code, methods, results, challenges)
- **Education-First**: Teach deliberation, not just sell software
- **Community-Driven**: Build with users, not for them
- **Evidence-Based**: Show data, research, case studies
- **Mission-Driven**: Lead with impact on democracy, not features
